# Chapter 3

They decided to search near the big oak tree, where some children were enjoying a game of tag. As they approached, Leo exclaimed, “Look, those kids are laughing! Maybe they know something about a magic wand!”

“Hey, kids!” Anna called out. “Have you seen a magic wand around here?”

One little girl, wearing a crown made of flowers, looked at them. “Wands? No, but we found a turtle that can dance! Want to see?”

“Of course!” Anna beamed.

The kids gathered around as the little girl clapped her hands and a turtle began to move its tiny feet to the rhythm of jazz music from a nearby band. Everyone burst into laughter, including Leo, who tried to dance along but ended up in a funny tumble. 

After the turtle dance, the kids shook their heads. “No wands here, just a dancing turtle!” said another boy, who looked very proud of their discovery.

“That was amazing!” Anna laughed. “Thanks anyway!” 

Next, they hopped over to the fountain, where an artist was painting. Anna asked him, “Have you seen a magic wand? I really need one to become a great magician!”

The artist smiled and said, “I might have seen something shiny by the lily pads. That could be it, but you might get wet!”

“Wet, schmet!” Leo croaked excitedly. “I’m a frog! Splish-splashing is my middle name!”
